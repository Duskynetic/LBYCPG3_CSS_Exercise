Details

First released for the PC in 2004 by Nihon Falcom in Japan, The Legend of Heroes: Trails in the Sky FC was the first release of the iconic Trails trilogy that would later became the prologue of what eventually became known as the Legend of Heroes series. Its first English version was released by the North American video game company Xseed Games 5 years after its port to the PlayStation Portable in 2006.



The Trails series has been hailed as one of the longest, largest, and most intricate turn-based JRPG series in history, only further boosting its popularity with the English releases of its second chapter in 2015 and third chapter in 2017.

It is currently available to play on PC on Steam, and has even been ported in high-definition on the PlayStation 3 and the PlayStation Portable. A remaster of the game was also introduced to the PS Vita back in 2015, but is only available in Japan.

Its large cast of playable characters, creative world design, and unrivaled level of combat customization has quickly allowed it to become Nihon Falcom's most popular and best-selling franchise to date.

Synopsis

The events of the game mostly revolve around the lives of two protagonists: Estelle Bright and her adopted brother, Joshua. They start their journey completing their training to become fully affiliated members of the bracer guild, a non-governmental organization that serves to maintain the peace and protect civillians in the continent through various means. One day, their father suddenly disappears aboard an airline that ends up going missing. The two then leave their hometown to embark on a journey around the kingdom of Liberl to investigate the incident while further progressing their ranks from junior to senior bracers. They create new friendships and experience new conflicts along the way as they face the different challenges awaiting them along the way, using the feelings of those around them to unfold a new generation.

Setting

Fifty years prior to where the game first begins, in the vast continent of Zemuria, came the dawn of the Orbal Revolution, which skyrocketed the innovations in technology that humanity was capable of producing thanks to what is known as orbal energy. This energy originates from mystical objects known as orbments, an ancient artifact discovered by a scientist in the middle of resarching a long-gone civilization. This leads to a massive power shift in societies throughout the entire continent, upheaving the political balance that were dominated previously by monarchies and aristocracies as a new, innovative method of acquiring such magical power can now be obtained by any city that has adept enough scientists to do so. Thus, the advent of orbal technology makes it possible for normal citizens to go through their everyday lives with much ease; whether they are travelling to different cities, fending off the different monsters roaming the world, or creating public facilities to improve their own quality of life.

With their small world suddenly entering their own version of the industrial evolution within the span of a human lifetime, Zemurian society enters an inevitable state of chaos. The rapid advancement in transportation completely transforms the nation's state of trade, turning simple merchants into economic juggernauts. The companies creating these orbments have become more relied upon by the masses than their own governments. Where only a mere few decades ago, upward social mobility seemed virtually impossible, is now essentially the norm; and this does not sit well with the people and organizations that have remained in power prior to this technological evolution. Non-democratic nations that are ruled by aristocrats and royalty become dwarfed by mega corporations economically. This is the underlying concept that drives the story forward as its protagonists go about their lives unaware of such problems brewing beneath the surface.
