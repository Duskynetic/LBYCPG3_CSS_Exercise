Breathtaking Music

It is almost common knowledge that the OST is a "make-or-break" aspect in any game. A good auditory experience is just as important at getting the player immersed in the world as the actual gameplay. Nihon Falcom knocks this out of the park with a wide range of soundtracks with different genres befitting the areas in which they are played.

The game's ED entitled Hoshi no Arika (星の在り処), which is translated to The Whereabouts of Stars in English, is a somber masterpiece that plays well with the visual of the game summing up the events that had happened prior as well as teasing what's to come for the sequel. Nihon Falcom specifically wrote the song for the game itself. Along with most songs in the soundtrack, it pays attention to the context with care and further deepens player immersion the further players get into the story.

Unique 2.5D Artstyle

Trails in the Sky boasts a unique way of depicting its world with the use of somewhat three-dimensional game assets with two-dimensional character sprites not commonly used in games in the similar genre during its time.

The game camera constantly maintains focus on the player and their party of characters for majority of the game. To adjust on which the game is displayed, an option is available for players to rotate the camera view 45 degrees at a time, providing a comprehensive view of each area that sells the perspective of an enthusiastic tourist looking around an unknown city more than it usually would have without this viewing feature.

Deep and Engaging Lore

The lore surrounding the Trails series is jam-packed with storytelling and does an excellent job introducing the player to thier world without it feeling too forced. They manage to create a world mixed with fantasy, steampunk, and anime elements all caught up in a land slowly building up political and economic turmoil due to a rapidly growing technological age.

While it can be said that a number of aspects in the story aren't all that original, especially at this day and age, their execution of these aspects provide a comprehensive and fulfilling experience that rivals most stories in fiction as whole even today.

Rich Worldbuilding

One of the most critically acclaimed things that Trails in the Sky has been known for is its unmatched quality of their game worlds in terms of the most important aspect that makes a game feel alive and realistic: its people.

Trails managed to create a vast world wherein every character has their own names and personalities that aren't connected to the main character. Every major city upholds its own unique political and economic agendas. Dialogue for every NPC changes as they live their lives parallel to the main character's own progression throughout the game. The developers made sure that no detail is ever trivialized and it has been nothing short of astounding.

Complex and Flexible Combat

The turn-based combat of Trails isn't uncommon in the JRPG world. It has been used aplenty in even the most well-known RPGs of today like Final Fantasy VII, South Park: The Stick of Truth, and many more.

What makes Trails stand out above the rest is its highly customizable magic system and character-unique skills that not only make your team stronger, but also sometimes directly influence the battle order, giving the player an edge at crucial moments for when it is needed. The complexity of combat also pays homage to the factor of random status effects that affect specific characters based on the turn order. It can be tedious to take in for a while but it pays off quickly due to how players can have not only flexible team compositions, but flexible roles for team members as well.
